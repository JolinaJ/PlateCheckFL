# PlateCheck Privacy Policy

_Last updated: July 19, 2026_

PlateCheck displays official government restaurant inspection records
inline on Google Search result pages.

## What PlateCheck collects

**Nothing.** PlateCheck does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any
user data of any kind.

- **No browsing history.** All restaurant matching happens locally in
  your browser against a bundled index of public records. The names you
  search for and the pages you visit never leave your device.
- **No analytics or telemetry.** The extension contains no tracking,
  measurement, or advertising code.
- **No accounts.** There is nothing to sign up for.

## Network requests

PlateCheck makes network requests only when you click "Show violations"
on an inspection card, and only to the official government source for
that record:

- **Florida:** `myfloridalicense.com` (Florida DBPR) — fetches the
  official inspection detail page for the record shown.
- **New York City:** `data.cityofnewyork.us` (NYC Open Data) — fetches
  the official DOHMH violation records for the inspection shown.

These requests contain only the record identifiers from the public
dataset (e.g. a license or inspection ID). They never include your
identity, search terms, or page content.

## Data sources

All inspection data shown is from official public records published by
the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR)
and the New York City Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (DOHMH).
